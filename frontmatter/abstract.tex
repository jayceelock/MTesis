\begin{abstract}[english]%===================================================

There are a large number of ways available to reduce the cost of a heliostat field in a concentrating solar power plant (CSP) plant. One way is to redesign the heliostat calibration procedure where the heliostats can be calibrated more frequently with comparable accuracy to current methods, without affecting the plant's power output or efficiency. This would reduce the need for precision actuators and sensors for each heliostat.

The Solar Thermal Energy Research Group (STERG) is attempting to task autonomous unmanned aerial vehicles (UAVs) to perform the calibration. However, the pose estimation accuracy of each UAV is required so that it can be integrated into the calibration model. Here, the pose refers to the six-dimensional vector describing an object's position and orientation.

\end{abstract}
